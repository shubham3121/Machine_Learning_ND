Implementation: Outlier Detection¶

Detecting outliers in the data is extremely important in the data preprocessing step of any analysis. The presence of outliers can often skew results which take into consideration these data points. There are many "rules of thumb" for what constitutes an outlier in a dataset. Here, we will use Tukey's Method for identfying outliers: An outlier step is calculated as 1.5 times the interquartile range (IQR). A data point with a feature that is beyond an outlier step outside of the IQR for that feature is considered abnormal.

In the code block below, you will need to implement the following:

  • Assign the value of the 25th percentile for the given feature to Q1. Use np.percentile for this.
  • Assign the value of the 75th percentile for the given feature to Q3. Again, use np.percentile.
  • Assign the calculation of an outlier step for the given feature to step.
  • Optionally remove data points from the dataset by adding indices to the outliers list.

NOTE: If you choose to remove any outliers, ensure that the sample data does not contain any of these points!
Once you have performed this implementation, the dataset will be stored in the variable good_data.

Question 6¶

What are the advantages to using a K-Means clustering algorithm? What are the advantages to using a Gaussian Mixture Model clustering algorithm? Given your observations about the wholesale customer data so far, which of the two algorithms will you use and why?

Answer: K-Means clustering partitions the clusters into k clusters using some distance metric. Generally, the distance metric is the Euclidean distance metric. The advantages of K-Means are:

  • It is simple and easy to implement.
  • Easy to interpret the clustering results.
  • Fast and efficient in terms of computational cost i.e. O(KN)
  • Does not require any modelling/ training of data.

Gausssian Mixture Model or Expectation Maximization Clustering is a type of soft clustering. This algorithm estimates a cluster 'i' by estimating the maximum liklihood that the data point, with mean , came from the cluster 'i'. This is in turn is done by maximization of the mean of the data point. Advantages of EM are:

  • It is simple, robust and easy to implementation.
  • It is immune to the effects of hard clustering, since each datapoint has some probability of belonging to a cluster.

Since we can see in the biplot we cannot easily different between the two clusters since there are many data points that are close to the boundary of the two clusters. Hence using KMeans clustering, which is a hard clustering alogrithm, may get stuck in case of ties. But using EM clustering overcomes this problem since each datapoint has some probability of belonging to some cluster.

How can the wholesale distributor use the customer segments to determine which customers, if any, would react positively to the change in delivery service?
Hint: Can we assume the change affects all customers equally? How can we determine which group of customers it affects the most?

Answer: As indicated A/B tests are a great way in determining the positive or negative impact of a new introduced service. For our case the wholesale distributor can make use of customer segments to determine whether a change in delivery service will have a positive or negative impact on the distribution. Since the customer segments represent two establishments, hence we cannot assume that the change will affect all customers equally. The wholesale distributor can produce a random set of customers which will contain equal customers, chosen at random, from each of the two customer Segements. Now the distributor can apply both its 5 day and 3 day service on this new set of customers. A change in the number of customers in each cluster would act as an indicator.

So, for example, if a customer of Segment 0, who buys more 'Fresh' and 'Frozen' products, if he is satified by the change in the delivery service then, he buys the same or increased amount of these products and there will no change in the number of customers in the cluster segement 0. But, if he is not satisfied, then he would decrease his expenditure on his products. As a result he will either get shifted to another cluster, where the purchase of 'Fresh' and 'Frozen' products is low(i.e. to customer Segement 1) or will diminish from the dataset. A similar resonining can be given for customer Segement 1.

Hence a positive response for the delivery would be indicated if the number of customers in a cluster remains same or increases and a negative response would be indicated by a decrease in the number of customers in that cluster. But, since the two customer segments represent different customers with different product preference, hence a postive response for customers of one segement may be negative response for the customers of the second segment. This problem can be overcome by applying the 5 day delivery test to customer segments on which it has positive reponse, while applying the 3 day delivery on the customers on which it gets a positive response.
